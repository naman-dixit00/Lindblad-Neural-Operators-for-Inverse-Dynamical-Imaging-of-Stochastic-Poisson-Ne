The Fokker–Planck equation provides a deterministic macro-level description of a system, whereas the Langevin equation provides a micro-level trajectory description of individual particles.If an individual particle's motion is governed by the Itô stochastic differential equation:\(dx_{t}=\mu (x_{t},t)dt+\sigma (x_{t},t)dW_{t}\)Where \(W_{t}\) is standard Brownian motion, the probability density function of all such particles evolves exactly according to the Fokker–Planck equation by setting:D₁(x, t) = μ(x, t)\(D_2(x, t) = \frac{1}{2}\sigma^2(x, t)\)
